Game Plan vs. Pitcher #6 — CSU Pueblo
Before the At-Bat
Expect Down-Outside First Pitch 38% of ABs open with DO. It's going to be low and away — take it unless it's really in your hitting zone. Do not chase it down.
Know Your AB Profile If she opens with CH (18% of ABs), the AB will be CH-heavy. Adjust timing from pitch one and stay ready for changeup throughout. If she opens with I, expect DO as the next pitch (I→DO: 8x).
Changeup Opens 18% of ABs — Unusual Most pitchers never open with a changeup. When #6 does, she's committed to using it throughout. Stay back and let the ball travel to you.
During the At-Bat
After DO → Protect Inside Immediately DO→I is the most common sequence (17x). Down-outside then inside is the signature pattern. As soon as you see DO, move your hands to cover the inner half for the next pitch.
1-1 Count → Sit on Changeup (42%) The most predictable single count. At 1-1, changeup comes 42% of the time — more than any other pitch. Slow your timing down at 1-1.
After CH → Look for DO (55%) After any changeup, down-outside follows 55% of the time. Recognized the CH, reset your timing, and look low and away for the next pitch.
Hitter's Count Approach
3-0 and 3-1 = Inside (Not a Cookie) This is the most important reversal from standard expectation. At 3-0 (100%) and 3-1 (57%), #6 goes inside — not down the middle. Do not sit on a hittable strike in hitter's counts. Protect the inner half.
2-0 → Inside (80%) After falling behind 2-0, next pitch is inside 80% of the time. Same warning — hitter's counts do not mean easy pitches down the middle from #6.
2-2 → Down-Outside (55%) The one even-pressure count that is readable: 55% DO at 2-2. Know that a 2-2 count means a likely low-away pitch. Make her throw it for a strike.
Where Damage Is Possible
Outside Pitch = Extra Base Risk The HR and multiple doubles came on outside pitches. When the count is in your favor and you get an outside pitch, drive it. That's where #6 is vulnerable.
Falls Behind in 58% of ABs — Work the Count More than half of ABs reach a behind count. Be patient. The deeper you get in the count, the more predictable she becomes — DO at 2-2 (55%), I at 3-1 (57%).
7 Walks in 50 ABs — Patience Pays 14% walk rate. Combined with HBP, she puts runners on without contact in 14% of ABs. Taking pitches and working counts forces mistakes.
